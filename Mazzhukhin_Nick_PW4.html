What is selector’s specificity and who it’s calculated?

Specificity refers to the weight or importance of a selector in determining which styles apply to an element. It’s calculated based on the combination of different types of selectors used in a rule. The following factors contribute to specificity (in order of importance): Inline styles: Highest specificity; ID selectors: Next highest; Class selectors, pseudo-classes, and attribute selectors: Medium specificity; Type selectors (lowest specificity) and universal selectors.

What are complex selectors?

Complex selectors allow us to target elements more precisely by combining different selectors.

What is the difference between pseudo-classes and pseudo-elements?

Pseudo-classes Define special states of elements, such as :hover. They don’t point to an element directly but rather describe its state. Pseudo-elements, like ::before, Represent parts of an element. They create virtual elements that don’t exist in the DOM.
